## Purpose

This document formally closes the Phase 66 simulation pipeline definition.

It confirms that simulation has been fully specified, bounded, and
explicitly limited to hypothetical, non-authoritative use.

This checkpoint prevents scope creep and unintended elevation of simulation
into decision-making or execution domains.

---

## Confirmed Scope

Simulation in Phase 66 is strictly limited to:

- hypothetical (*what-if*) scenario exploration,
- descriptive analysis of possible outcomes,
- explicit surfacing of assumptions and uncertainties,
- non-ranked, non-prescriptive result presentation.

Simulation does NOT possess and MUST NOT acquire:

- decision authority,
- recommendation authority,
- execution capability,
- optimization goals,
- autonomous behavior.

## Boundary Enforcement

Any attempt to:

- extend simulation into recommendation,
- convert simulation output into implicit decisions,
- integrate simulation with execution paths,
- centralize simulation results into an agent-like process,

constitutes a Phase 66 violation and MUST:

1. halt immediately,
2. explicitly identify the boundary breach,
3. escalate to governance authority.

No silent expansion or reinterpretation is permitted.

## Relationship to Other Domains

- Observability supplies factual signals.
- Simulation explores hypothetical interpretations.
- Authority and execution remain external.

Simulation MUST remain informational only.
